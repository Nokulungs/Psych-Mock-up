"A space to slow down and make sense of what may be happening."

You don't have to figure it out alone. Together, we look deeper.

At times, life can feel overwhelming, confusing, or difficult to navigate. You may find yourself facing challenges that seem to endure despite your best efforts, struggling in relationships, feeling disconnected from yourself, or carrying emotions that don't quite make sense.

Therapy is a space to slow down and make sense of what may be happening. Together, we can explore with curiosity and care, developing a deeper understanding of what may be contributing to distress and what might support meaningful change.

Seeking therapy is not only for times of crisis. It can also be an opportunity to get to know yourself better, strengthen your relationships, and feel more present in your own life.

01 / Functional Specialties

Areas of Clinical Work

Anxiety, panic, and ongoing worry

Navigating emotional intensity, constant overwhelm, or feeling "stuck" in repetitive behavioral cycles. We work to break down the internal stress responses to restore agency.

Low mood, depression, and feeling emotionally shut down

Addressing persistent low motivation, feelings of detachment, fatigue, and major life transitions that alter how you connect with everyday environments.

Trauma and the lingering effects of difficult experiences

Processing deep-seated historical trauma or recent distressing events within a structured, safe clinical dynamic designed to unpack experiences safely.

Confusion in relationships or identity questions

Evaluating repeated patterns in partnerships, complications in self-worth, and establishing clearer structural boundary systems within personal networks.

Psychological support for healthcare workers & burnout

Specialized containment strategies for frontline professionals experiencing compassion fatigue, systemic stress, and executive performance exhaustion.

02 / Theoretical Grounding

Grounded in curiosity. Held with care.

I am a Clinical Psychologist whose work is informed by psychodynamic psychotherapy and a deep respect for the complexity of human experience. I believe that psychological difficulties deserve thoughtful attention rather than quick assumptions or simple explanations.


Sometimes we find ourselves repeating patterns, feeling stuck, or reacting in ways that do not fully make sense. I aim to offer a space that feels calm, respectful, and collaborative, where your story can be heard and explored at a pace that feels right for you.
