Psychotherapy

At times, life can feel overwhelming, confusing, or difficult to navigate. You may find yourself facing challenges that seem to endure despite your best efforts, struggling in relationships, feeling disconnected from yourself, or carrying emotions that don't quite make sense.

Therapy is a space to slow down and make sense of what may be happening. Together, we can explore with curiosity and care, developing a deeper understanding of what may be contributing to distress and what might support meaningful change.

Seeking therapy is not only for times of crisis. It can also be an opportunity to get to know yourself better, strengthen your relationships, and feel more present in your own life.

Areas I Can Help With

I work with a range of emotional and psychological difficulties, and more complex presentations, including:

Anxiety, panic, and ongoing worry

Low mood, depression, and feeling emotionally shut down

Trauma and the lingering effects of difficult experiences

Emotional intensity, overwhelm, or feeling "stuck" in patterns

Confusion in relationships or repeated relational difficulties

Experiences that feel hard to explain or make sense of

Stress, burnout, and major life transitions

Questions around identity, self-worth, and direction

Psychological support for healthcare workers

My Therapeutic Approach 

My approach is based on the understanding that our experiences, relationships, and ways of coping are shaped by both our past and our present.

Sometimes we find ourselves repeating patterns, feeling stuck, or reacting in ways that do not fully make sense. While these experiences can be frustrating, they often carry important information about our emotional world and can begin to make sense when we have space to explore them more closely.

Therapy provides an opportunity to explore these experiences in a thoughtful and supportive environment. Rather than only focusing on what feels difficult, we try to understand what your experiences might be telling us about you, your relationships, and what you've been carrying over time.

I aim to offer a space that feels calm, respectful, and collaborative, where your story can be heard and explored at a pace that feels right for you.

About

I am a Clinical Psychologist registered with the HPCSA and BHF.

I strive to create a therapeutic space that is warm, reflective, and grounded in genuine curiosity about each person's unique story.
